On A Mirror


As a young boy, I felt the pain,
The sorrows that I have gained.
But I also smiled like a child,
Feeling the thrills and joys of life.

I reveled in the pleasure and dedication,
Endured the bits of agony and affliction.
In the mirror, I saw myself,
A reflection of who I was and am.

But as I grew older and faced the strife,
I learned that life is a mix of loss and life.
Though I have lived it to the fullest,
The precious things I lost never returned.

No more sorrow, no more pain,
No more agony or affliction, no more strain.
Yet no joy, no thrill, no pleasure,
No more dedication or treasure.

All that's left is desolation, dejection,
Desperation, and the weight of demotivation.
Now, in the mirror, I see a stranger,
Someone I didn't know, an unfamiliar danger.

After he came through my soul,
The hues of my life had already flown.
Everything felt out of control,
I began to feel like an error unknown.

Now, I no longer want to see my reflection,
For the stranger in the mirror shows no connection.
The changes I've experienced, I now accept,
And I'll continue on, until the end, with few regrets.

By Zwe Nyi Naing
